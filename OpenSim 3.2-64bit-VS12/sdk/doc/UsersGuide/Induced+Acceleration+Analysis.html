OpenSim Documentation : Induced Acceleration Analysis

This page last changed on Feb 07, 2013 by jenhicks.

The Induced Acceleration Analysis is used to compute accelerations caused or "induced" by individual forces acting on a model, for example, the contribution of individual muscle forces to the mass center acceleration. Typically, induced accelerations of generalized coordinates (e.g., knee angle) or body locations (e.g., model's center of mass) are desired, and the forces consist of muscles, gravity, and any additional forces (e.g., residual actuators, reserve actuators, etc.).  The topics covered in this section are:
